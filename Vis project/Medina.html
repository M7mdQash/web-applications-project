Medina Tourism

Medina is a city of peace and tranquility. It is home to the Prophet's Mosque, which is the second holiest site in Islam. The city's historical and religious sites offer a deep insight into Islamic history..

Al Masjed Al nabawy

Al-Masjid an-Nabawi, or the Prophet's Mosque, is a magnificent structure that stands as a symbol of Islamic architecture. The exterior of the mosque showcases intricate designs and the spiritual significance it holds for Muslims worldwide.

Masjed Qubaa

Masjid Quba is the first mosque built by Prophet Muhammad in Medina. It holds historical significance and is known for its unique architecture. Pilgrims often visit this mosque seeking blessings and spiritual solace.

Uhud mountain

Mount Uhud, located just north of Medina, is a mountain steeped in history. It was the site of the Battle of Uhud, a significant event in Islamic history. Today, the mountain serves as a symbol of resilience and faith, attracting visitors for its panoramic views and historical importance.

Al Malek Fahad complex

The King Fahd Complex for the Printing of the Holy Quran is a prominent institution dedicated to producing and distributing copies of the Holy Quran. Located in Medina, it plays a crucial role in ensuring the widespread availability of the Quran to Muslims around the world.
